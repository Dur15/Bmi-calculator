BMI Guide

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a value derived from mass (weight) and height. It's used to categorize weight ranges that may lead to health problems.

  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5
  • Normal weight: 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight: 25–29.9
  • Obese: BMI ≥ 30

How to Calculate

The formula is: BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))² For imperial units: BMI = [weight (lbs) / (height (in))²] × 703

Health Implications

BMI correlates with body fat and health risks:

  • High BMI → Increased risk of heart disease, diabetes
  • Low BMI → Nutritional deficiencies, osteoporosis

How accurate is your BMI calculator?

Our BMI calculator uses the standard WHO formula and is accurate to within 0.1 BMI points. All calculations are performed locally in your browser - we never store or transmit your personal data. The calculator supports both metric (cm/kg) and imperial (inches/lbs) units.

What's the difference between BMI and Body Fat calculator?

BMI Calculator: Uses only height and weight to estimate body fat. Quick and easy but doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat.

Body Fat Calculator: Uses the U.S. Navy Method with neck, waist, and hip measurements to provide a more accurate body composition estimate. This is better for athletes and fitness enthusiasts.

How does your Calorie Counter work?

Our calorie calculator uses three scientifically validated formulas:

  • Mifflin-St Jeor: Most accurate for general population
  • Harris-Benedict: Traditional formula, good for reference
  • Katch-McArdle: Most accurate if you know your body fat %

It calculates your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) and TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) based on your activity level and goals.
